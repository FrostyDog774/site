Okay but what is an A Grade pin?

So I only sell A and B grade pins, every pin is handmade in a way, like enamel is filled with hands and the screen print process is also handled by hands, so every pin HAS TO have imperfections, well that's life if you try hard enough you can find imperfections in everything. Does that mean pins are life? Maybe. Maybe not.
A grade means everything's good with the pin, it turned out just as planned (Death Note intensifies) and there is no super visible problems with it. Great for collecting and displaying.
B grades on the other hand, has imperfections (like stray glitter, under-filled enamel, scratches, like anything visible) and they are discounted. B grades are more suitable for people who wear their pins a lot and has a habit to throw their bags to walls and floors and sometimes ceilings (again, me).
I don't sell C grades (which has really big imperfections).
